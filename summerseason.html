Eternal September

The campus calls out for occupation. Its hollow cavities seek to shelter you. It beckons you to consume its offerings, and in turn, sustain it. Will you come at its request, or remain exposed to the wild irrational winds of the outer world.

All of the fire pits around campus are lit this evening.

You smell the angel’s trumpet blooming in a final attempt to replicate before its flowers die and it conserves its energies for next year.

The music boys are covering all of Neutral Milk Hotel’s “In The Aeroplane Over The Sea” in order again. They might get through it without looking up the lyrics this time, although of course their voices still won’t crack quite right when they get to the mid section of “Oh Comely”.

A couple of alignment researchers play Go by the cabinet of curiosities as they argue about whether the control paradigm would help in the real world. You know that it makes no sense to judge who is right about the argument based on who is winning the game, but you can’t help it. You remind yourself that the agent foundations researcher gave the MATS scholar a handicap of four.

A controversial popular internet personality is talking to a molecular biology PhD about what it would take to get hundreds of thousands of human genomes into a dataset so that they could make superbabies. They are considering a product that tells you which celebrity you are genetically closest to. You begin to doubt your own sanity as you start to think that their plan might not only work, but that it might be the most important thing you could work on.

FAQ

Can I invite my team?

Yes. Depending on your team size you may want to rent a larger office. (Each additional person should also pay for general access, but you only pay once for the office)

Can I bring my family?

Yes. We'll be working to make sure there's space where children can hang out, and I expect we can find you a cluster of bedrooms that meet your needs. (Each additional person should also pay for general access. We are figuring out pricing for children, most likely ~50% of general access)

Will Lightcone team be running a bunch of programming for this?

Maybe some, but mostly we'll be focused on maintaining basic infrastructure for the campus (and for LessWrong). But, over the past few months we've found the people organically staying here to have a good time – sometimes hosting events on their own, sometimes just enjoying quiet spaciousness.

I already live in Berkeley. Is there a point to coming?

Well, the depends on you and what you find valuable! But, here are reasons you might want to come:

  • Experiment with working on the spacious Lighthaven campus.
  • Try out living in our soundproof, lightproof bedrooms and see how you like it.
  • Come for a mini vacation. Take a break from your usual life.
  • Visit with your significant other for a romantic gateway.
  • Host social events at Lighthaven (discussions, impromptu workshops, games and dances)

Can I bring guests for a day?

If you've paid for at least a week of space-access, you can invite people over for the day. (If you want to bring them over multiple times, check in with us first)

This, uh feels kinda last minute?

Yep, it is. We managed to book most of the first nine months of next year, but weren't able to book much for the following four weeks. And so we were like "Hey, we want to test out having a flourishing campus/village/thingy without committing to maintaining it long term, and the venue will be booked out for events soon. Seemed like now was a good time to test out Lighthaven as a temporary campus and see how it went."
